Nuclear Strike allows a Ghost to call down a nuclear missile on a target location, dealing 500 damage or two-thirds of a unit's maximum HP (whichever is greater) across a large blast radius. The Ghost must remain stationary and alive during the roughly 10-second launch sequence. A red targeting dot is visible to all players during this countdown. Requires a Nuclear Silo addon on a Command Center with an armed nuke (200/200 and 8 supply to produce).

In competitive play, Nuclear Strike is used more for area denial and psychological pressure than for direct kills. The threat of a nuke forces the opponent to either evacuate the targeted area or frantically hunt the Ghost, buying time and disrupting their positioning. In TvZ, nukes can force Zerg to abandon a contested base or pull Defilers out of position. Some players use the targeting sequence as a distraction for a simultaneous attack elsewhere on the map.

The most common mistake is overinvesting in nukes — the 200/200 cost and the infrastructure required (Ghost, Academy, Covert Ops, Nuclear Silo) make them expensive distractions. To land nukes effectively, use multiple Ghosts and cloak them before targeting. Place the nuke slightly behind the enemy's position so they retreat into it rather than away from it.
