Cloaking Field renders the casting unit invisible to all enemy units that lack detection. While active, cloaking continuously drains energy at a rate of approximately 0.9 energy per frame, giving a limited window of invisibility before the unit's energy is depleted. Activation costs 25 energy. Available to both Ghosts (researched at Covert Ops) and Wraiths (researched at Control Tower), with separate research required for each unit type.

In competitive play, cloaking serves distinct purposes depending on the unit. Cloaked Wraiths are a potent harassment tool in TvT and TvZ — they can pick off Overlords, workers, and isolated units without being targeted, forcing the opponent to invest in detection. Wraith cloak openings in TvT are a classic build that can end games if the opponent fails to get a Comsat or Missile Turrets in time. Cloaked Ghosts are used primarily to set up Nuclear Strikes or Lockdown without being killed before casting.

Managing cloaking energy is crucial — decloak when not actively in danger to conserve energy for when it matters. A common mistake is leaving cloak active while retreating to safety, draining energy that could be needed for the next engagement. Against opponents without mobile detection, cloaked units can dominate the map. Always keep spare energy for emergency cloaking if your Wraith or Ghost comes under threat. Remember that Comsat scans, Observers, Overlords with detection upgrade, Missile Turrets, Photon Cannons, and Spore Colonies can all reveal cloaked units.
