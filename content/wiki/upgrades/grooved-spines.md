Grooved Spines increases Hydralisk attack range by 1 (from 4 to 5), giving them significantly better reach in combat. The extra range allows Hydralisks to begin firing sooner when engaging, to attack from behind other units in formation, and to kite melee units more effectively. The range increase applies to both ground and air attacks, improving Hydralisk anti-air capability as well.

In competitive play, Grooved Spines is critical for Hydralisk effectiveness. Without it, Hydralisks must close to dangerously short range before firing, exposing them to splash damage and melee attacks. The extra range means more Hydralisks in a formation can fire simultaneously, dramatically increasing the army's effective DPS. In ZvP, ranged Hydralisks can engage Dragoons on more favorable terms. In ZvT, the range helps Hydralisks contribute damage from behind Lurker positions and avoid being caught by Siege Tank splash.

Grooved Spines is researched at the Hydralisk Den, always alongside Muscular Augments in Hydralisk builds. Both upgrades begin as soon as the Den completes, typically around 7-9 minutes. Skipping this upgrade when using Hydralisks is virtually never done — the range disadvantage without it is too severe.
