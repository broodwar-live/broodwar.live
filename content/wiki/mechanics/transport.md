## Overview

Transport units allow ground forces to be airlifted across the map, bypassing terrain obstacles, walls, and enemy defensive lines. **Drop play** — loading units into transports and unloading them in the enemy's base or behind their army — is one of the most powerful and exciting tactics in Brood War, enabling harassment, multi-pronged attacks, and devastating surprise strikes.

## Transport Units by Race

### Protoss — Shuttle
- Costs 200 minerals
- 8 cargo space
- Relatively fragile (80 HP, 60 shields)
- The Shuttle is central to one of BW's most iconic micro techniques: **Shuttle-Reaver** play

### Terran — Dropship
- Costs 100 minerals, 100 gas
- 8 cargo space
- 150 HP, no shields
- Commonly used for Marine/Medic drops and Siege Tank drops behind enemy lines

### Zerg — Overlord
- Requires **Ventral Sacs** upgrade (researched at Lair) to carry units
- 8 cargo space
- Very slow without the **Pneumatized Carapace** speed upgrade
- Doubles as supply provider and detector, making Overlord drops resource-efficient but slow to set up

## Cargo Capacity

Each transport has **8 cargo space**. Units occupy different amounts of space based on their size:

| Unit Size | Cargo Space | Examples |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Small     | 1          | Zergling, Marine, Zealot, Firebat |
| Medium    | 2          | Hydralisk, Vulture, Lurker, Ghost, High Templar |
| Large     | 4          | Siege Tank, Reaver, Ultralisk, Dragoon |

This means a single transport can carry 8 Marines, 4 Hydralisks, or 2 Siege Tanks. Unit size for transport purposes sometimes differs from combat size classification.

## Key Drop Strategies

### Shuttle-Reaver (Protoss)
The most mechanically demanding drop technique. The Shuttle loads a Reaver, flies to the target, unloads, the Reaver fires a Scarab, and the Shuttle immediately reloads the Reaver before it can be killed. Expert players cycle this rapidly, dealing massive damage while keeping the fragile Reaver alive. This technique defined many professional Protoss careers.

### Marine/Medic Drop (Terran)
Two Dropships carrying Marines and Medics land in the enemy's mineral line or tech area. Stimmed Marines deal enormous damage quickly, and Medics keep them alive. The goal is to force the opponent to pull their army back, relieving pressure on the front.

### Siege Tank Drop (Terran)
Dropping Siege Tanks on high ground or behind enemy lines. Once sieged, Tanks deal devastating splash damage to anything nearby. Particularly effective when combined with a frontal assault that splits the opponent's attention.

### Overlord Drop (Zerg)
Slower to execute due to Overlord speed, but effective for dropping Lurkers or Zerglings into enemy bases. Lurker drops are especially punishing because the opponent needs both detection and ground forces to respond.

## Counterplay

Drops are countered by:
- **Anti-air units and turrets** positioned near likely drop zones
- **Minimap awareness** to spot incoming transports early
- **Mobile army positioning** that can quickly rotate to defend multiple locations
- Killing transports before they unload — units inside a destroyed transport are killed instantly
