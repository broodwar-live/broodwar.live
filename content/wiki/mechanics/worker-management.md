## Overview

Workers are the economic backbone of every Brood War game. How you produce, distribute, and protect your workers directly determines your income rate and, by extension, your ability to build armies and tech. Worker management encompasses saturation, transfers, harassment defense, and even offensive worker tactics.

## Optimal Saturation

### Mineral Saturation
The ideal number of workers per mineral patch is **2 workers per patch** for optimal income. A typical base has 8 mineral patches, so **16 workers** is the saturation point. Additional workers beyond 16 still mine, but with significantly diminished returns — they spend most of their time waiting for patches to become available.

In practice, players often build 18-20 workers per base. The extra workers beyond saturation serve as insurance against worker losses from harassment and can be quickly transferred to new expansions.

### Gas Saturation
The optimal number of workers per gas geyser is **3**. With 3 workers, the geyser is nearly continuously mined. A 4th worker adds minimal additional income and is usually better used on minerals. However, on geysers with a long walk distance from the base structure, a 4th worker can provide a small benefit.

Each base has exactly 1 geyser, so a fully saturated base runs 16 mineral workers + 3 gas workers = **19 workers** at true optimal efficiency.

## Worker Transfers

When you expand to a new base, transferring workers from your saturated main to the new expansion immediately establishes income at the new location. Without transfers, you must wait for newly produced workers to slowly fill the expansion.

The standard technique is to **rally 6-8 workers** from your main base to the new expansion as soon as the base structure finishes. This instantly brings the expansion to partial saturation while you continue producing workers to fill both bases.

## Worker Harassment

Attacking the opponent's workers is one of the highest-value plays in Brood War. Killing a single worker costs the opponent not just the 50-mineral replacement cost, but also the mining time lost while training a replacement and the income that worker would have generated.

Common harassment techniques:
- **Mutalisk harass**: Mutalisks fly into mineral lines to pick off workers. Even killing 3-4 workers is a significant economic blow.
- **Vulture run-by**: Vultures sneak into the natural expansion and kill workers with their high speed and concussive damage.
- **Drop harass**: Dropping combat units directly into mineral lines (see Transport & Drop Play).
- **Dark Templar**: A single undetected DT in a mineral line can kill enormous numbers of workers before being dealt with.

## Worker Defense

When harassed, the decision of whether to pull workers to fight or flee is critical:

- Against a single unit (e.g., a lone Zealot), surrounding with workers can kill it quickly. Workers deal 5 damage per hit, and 10+ workers attacking simultaneously can overwhelm small raiding parties.
- Against splash damage (Reavers, Siege Tanks) or area spells (Psionic Storm), workers must be evacuated immediately — fighting back just adds to the casualties.
- **Mineral walking**: Workers ordered to mine a specific mineral patch can walk through other units. This can be exploited to escape surrounds or to slip workers past blockades.

## SCV Repair

Terran SCVs can repair mechanical units and buildings. This is a unique economic-military interaction:

- SCVs repairing Siege Tanks or Bunkers during a push extends their survivability enormously
- Repair costs resources (proportional to the unit's build cost), creating a trade-off between economy and sustain
- Multiple SCVs can repair the same target simultaneously for faster healing
- SCV repair micro at the front line is a hallmark of professional Terran play
