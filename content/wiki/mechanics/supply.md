## Overview

Supply is the population cap system in StarCraft: Brood War. Every unit occupies a certain number of supply slots, and you can only build units if you have enough supply available. The maximum supply cap is **200** for every race. Managing supply efficiently — building supply structures on time without overbuilding — is a core macro skill.

## Supply Providers

Each race builds supply differently:

- **Terran — Supply Depot**: Costs 100 minerals, provides 8 supply. A static building that is often used as part of wall-offs at the natural ramp.
- **Protoss — Pylon**: Costs 100 minerals, provides 8 supply. Also generates the psionic power field required for building placement. Losing a pylon can disable nearby buildings.
- **Zerg — Overlord**: Costs 100 minerals, provides 8 supply. A floating unit that also provides scouting vision. Losing overlords to anti-air reduces supply, potentially "supply blocking" the Zerg player.

## Starting Supply

Each race begins with some supply already provided:

- Terran: 10 supply (Command Center provides 10)
- Protoss: 9 supply (Nexus provides 9)
- Zerg: 9 supply (Hatchery provides 1, starting Overlord provides 8)

With 4 starting workers each costing 1 supply, players begin with 4/10 (Terran) or 4/9 (Protoss, Zerg) supply used.

## Supply Blocking

**Supply blocking** occurs when a player hits their current supply cap and cannot produce units until a new supply structure completes. This is one of the most common macro mistakes at all levels of play. Even a few seconds of being supply blocked can cost critical production cycles, especially in the early game where every unit counts.

Good players anticipate supply needs by building supply structures before they reach the cap. A common heuristic is to start a new supply provider when you are within 2-3 supply of your current cap.

## Unit Supply Costs

Supply costs vary widely. Small units like Zerglings cost 0.5 supply each (produced in pairs for 1 supply total), Marines cost 1, and large units like Carriers cost 6 and Battlecruisers cost 8. This means reaching the 200 supply cap can represent very different army sizes depending on composition — a Zergling army at 200 supply has far more individual units than a Carrier fleet at 200 supply.

## Strategic Implications

Supply cap means that cost-efficiency matters enormously in maxed-out army fights. A player who can trade efficiently — destroying more supply worth of enemy units than they lose — gains a decisive advantage. This is why late-game engagements in professional play are so carefully planned: a single bad trade at 200/200 can decide the game.
