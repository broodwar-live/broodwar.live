## Overview

Building placement in StarCraft: Brood War is far more than just putting structures down wherever they fit. Each race has unique placement rules, and the physical arrangement of buildings directly affects defensive strength, unit pathing, and strategic options. Mastering building placement — often called **sim-city** — is a fundamental skill.

## Race-Specific Rules

### Terran
- Buildings can be placed on any buildable terrain (no special requirements)
- Most buildings can **lift off** and fly to a new location, then land again. Exceptions: Addons (Machine Shop, Control Tower, Comsat Station, Physics Lab, Covert Ops) cannot lift off independently.
- Addons are built attached to the right side of their parent building. If there is not enough space to the right, the addon cannot be constructed.
- Lifted buildings can land at any valid location, even claiming another building's addon.

### Protoss
- All buildings (except Nexus and Assimilator) must be placed within a **Pylon's power field** — the blue aura that surrounds each Pylon.
- If a Pylon is destroyed, all buildings powered solely by that Pylon become **unpowered** and cease to function. They do not produce units, research, or provide any benefits until re-powered by building a new Pylon nearby.
- This creates a strategic vulnerability: sniping key Pylons can disable entire production clusters or defensive lines.

### Zerg
- All buildings (except Hatchery and Extractor) must be placed on **creep** — the purple organic surface that spreads from Hatcheries, Creep Colonies, and Nydus Canal exits.
- Creep gradually recedes when the building generating it is destroyed, potentially orphaning nearby structures (they continue to function but new buildings cannot be placed nearby).
- A Drone is **consumed** when morphing into a Zerg building — the Drone becomes the building, costing Zerg an additional 50 minerals in effective cost.

## Wall-Offs

**Wall-offs** are building arrangements that completely block a ramp or choke point, preventing enemy units from passing through. This is primarily a Terran and Protoss technique:

- **Terran wall-off**: Typically Supply Depot + Barracks at the natural ramp. Blocks Zergling run-bys in TvZ. The Barracks can lift off to allow friendly units through.
- **Protoss wall-off**: Forge + Gateway or Pylon + Gateway at the ramp. Common in PvZ to block early Zerglings while teching to Corsair or Reaver.

Wall-off building placement must be pixel-precise — a gap even one tile wide allows small units to slip through. Players practice specific wall patterns for each map's ramp geometry.

## Proxy Buildings

**Proxy** buildings are production structures built outside your main base, closer to (or hidden near) the opponent. Common proxy strategies include:

- **Proxy Barracks**: A Barracks built near the enemy base for faster Marine/Bunker rushes.
- **Proxy Gateway**: A Gateway placed aggressively for faster Zealot pressure.
- **Proxy Hatchery**: A Zerg Hatchery built at an expansion location very early, or even near the enemy base for a "proxy hatch" rush.

Proxy buildings trade economic efficiency for faster aggression by reducing unit travel time.

## Sim-City

**Sim-city** refers to the art of arranging buildings tightly and strategically within your base. Good sim-city:

- Creates natural walls and funnels that slow enemy units if they break in
- Protects key tech buildings behind production buildings
- Leaves room for unit movement and rally paths
- Avoids blocking addon placement (Terran) or creating Pylon dependency chains (Protoss)

Bad sim-city can lose games — a poorly placed building that blocks your army's retreat path, or a Pylon arrangement where destroying one Pylon disables half your tech, can be fatal.
