## Overview

Cloaking and detection form one of the most critical mechanics in StarCraft: Brood War. Cloaked and burrowed units are **completely invisible** to the enemy unless they have a detection source nearby. Failing to have detection available can result in instant game losses — a single undetected Dark Templar or Lurker can destroy an entire base.

## Cloaked Units

Cloaked units are invisible to the enemy but can still attack and move normally. They can only be seen (and therefore targeted) by units or buildings with detection.

### Permanently Cloaked
- **Dark Templar** (Protoss): Always cloaked. 40 damage per hit makes them devastating if undetected. A primary reason every race needs early detection in PvX matchups.
- **Observer** (Protoss): A cloaked flying detector. Cannot attack but provides vision and detection while being invisible itself.

### Ability-Based Cloaking
- **Wraith** (Terran): Cloaking Field ability (requires research). Drains energy while active. Used for harass and scouting.
- **Ghost** (Terran): Personnel Cloaking ability (requires research). Drains energy. Used to position for Lockdown or Nuclear Launch.

## Burrowed Units

Burrowing is primarily a Zerg mechanic (requires Burrow research at the Hatchery). Burrowed units are hidden underground, invisible to enemies without detection, but **cannot attack or move** while burrowed (with one exception).

### Units That Can Burrow
- **Zergling, Hydralisk, Drone, Defiler, Infested Terran**: Can burrow for concealment and ambush. They must unburrow to act.
- **Lurker**: The exception — Lurkers can **only attack while burrowed**. They fire subterranean spines that deal 20 splash damage in a line. Lurkers are useless above ground and deadly below it, making detection absolutely mandatory for pushing into Lurker positions.

## Detection Sources

Each race has access to mobile and static detection:

### Protoss
- **Observer**: Flying, cloaked, mobile detector. 9 sight range. The most versatile detector in the game — invisible to the enemy (unless they also have detection) and can be positioned anywhere.
- **Photon Cannon**: Static defense structure with detection. Useful for base defense but cannot protect an advancing army.

### Terran
- **Science Vessel**: Flying mobile detector. Also provides powerful spells (Irradiate, EMP, Defensive Matrix). Expensive and requires Starport + Science Facility.
- **Missile Turret**: Static anti-air defense with detection. Cheap and commonly built in mineral lines to spot burrowed/cloaked units.
- **Comsat Station** (Scanner Sweep): Temporarily reveals a large area anywhere on the map, including cloaked and burrowed units. Costs 50 energy. The fastest detection response available — no travel time required.

### Zerg
- **Overlord**: Flying unit that provides supply AND detection (once the Pneumatized Carapace upgrade is researched for speed, Overlords become effective mobile detectors even without the speed upgrade, all Overlords detect by default).
- **Spore Colony**: Static anti-air defense with detection.

## Detection Gaps

The most dangerous moment for any player is a **detection gap** — a period where they have no detection available. This commonly happens when:

- An Observer or Science Vessel is sniped at the start of a fight
- A Terran uses all their Comsat energy and faces immediate cloaked aggression
- Zerg Overlords are killed by anti-air before the ground army arrives

Professional players constantly track their opponent's detection and actively try to eliminate it before committing invisible units. Sniping Observers before a Dark Templar attack or killing Overlords before a Lurker push is a hallmark of high-level play.
