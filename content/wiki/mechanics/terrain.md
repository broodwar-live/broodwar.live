## Overview

Terrain in StarCraft: Brood War is not just cosmetic — it fundamentally shapes combat. Elevation differences, narrow choke points, unbuildable terrain, and water all affect unit movement, attack accuracy, and strategic options. Mastering terrain is essential to competitive play.

## High Ground Advantage

The most important terrain mechanic is the **high ground miss chance**. When a unit on low ground attacks a unit on high ground, the attacker has a **47% chance to miss** each shot — unless the attacker has direct vision of the high ground target.

This is an enormous defensive advantage. A few units positioned on high ground can hold off a much larger army attacking from below. The miss chance applies per shot, so it affects fast-attacking units (like Marines) and slow-attacking units (like Siege Tanks) equally in probabilistic terms, but the variance is more punishing for slow attackers who may miss critical shots.

### Gaining Vision

The miss chance is negated entirely if the attacking player has **vision** of the high ground. Sources of vision include:

- **Flying units**: Overlords, Observers, Science Vessels, any air unit
- **Detector abilities**: Comsat Scanner sweep
- **Units already on high ground**: If you have even one unit up the ramp, your other units below no longer suffer the miss chance against targets they can see
- **Buildings**: A building placed on high ground provides vision

This is why Observers and Overlords are often used as "spotters" during assaults up ramps.

## Choke Points

Ramps and narrow passages between cliffs create **choke points** — tight spaces that limit how many units can engage at once. Choke points are central to Brood War strategy:

- **Wall-offs**: Terran and Protoss frequently block ramp chokes with buildings to prevent early rushes, especially Zergling run-bys.
- **Lurker positioning**: A Lurker burrowed at a choke can deny passage to an entire army.
- **Tank siege lines**: Siege Tanks positioned behind a choke can devastate any force trying to push through.
- **Spell impact**: Area-of-effect spells like Psionic Storm are far more effective in choke points where units are forced to stack.

## Cliff Abuse

Ranged units can attack from cliff edges where melee units cannot reach them. This is called **cliff abuse** and is a critical micro technique. Common examples include:

- Dragoons firing down from cliffs at units that cannot path up to them
- Siege Tanks in siege mode positioned on high ground, bombarding units below
- Reavers dropping scarabs from cliff edges

## Unbuildable Terrain and Water

Not all flat ground is buildable. Some tiles are marked as unbuildable despite appearing passable. Water and deep space tiles block ground unit movement entirely, creating natural barriers that define map flow. Island expansions — mineral patches accessible only by air — are a direct consequence of water terrain.

## Map Design Impact

Professional map designers use terrain to balance racial matchups. Wider ramps favor Zerg (more room for swarms), while narrow ramps favor Terran and Protoss (easier to wall and defend). The number and width of attack paths, the presence of island expansions, and the elevation layout all contribute to whether a map favors aggressive or defensive play.
