A 4-player twilight map that holds the distinction of being one of the last maps used in OSL and MSL before those legendary leagues ended, used in professional competition from 2010 through 2012. The twilight tileset provides an atmospheric purple-blue setting, with four spawn positions arranged in rotational symmetry. Each main base connects to a natural expansion through a ramp with good chokepoint defense.

Grand Line's design represents the culmination of years of Korean mapmaking expertise. The layout is carefully balanced with multiple pathways between quadrants, ensuring no spawn position holds an advantage. The center features a mix of open terrain and elevated positions that create dynamic engagement scenarios, while the expansion layout provides a clear economic progression that rewards solid macro play.

Named after the ocean route in the manga One Piece, Grand Line was known for producing balanced, high-quality games during the twilight of Korean professional Brood War. The map played evenly across all matchups, reflecting the sophisticated balance testing that characterized late-era map design. Its appearance in the final seasons of OSL and MSL gives it a special place in competitive history — it was among the last battlefields where the greatest professional players competed in the original Korean league format. The map is remembered fondly as a fitting endnote to the golden age of Korean Brood War esports.
