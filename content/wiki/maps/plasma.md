A 4-player space platform map that was popular in the OSL during the golden era, used in professional competition from 2006 through 2008. Plasma features the dark metallic aesthetic of the space platform tileset, with four spawn positions connected by narrow bridges that create natural chokepoints throughout the map. The bridges are the map's defining structural element — they force armies into confined corridors where positioning and composition matter more than raw numbers.

The narrow bridge chokepoints fundamentally shape how games play out on Plasma. Siege tanks become extremely powerful when positioned at bridge entrances, as attacking armies must funnel through tight spaces to advance. Similarly, lurkers can hold bridges with devastating efficiency, and protoss zealots can create effective blockades in the narrow passages. This makes the map particularly interesting for defensive play and creates dramatic moments when one player finally commits to crossing a contested bridge.

Plasma appeared in multiple OSL seasons and produced games that were distinctly different from those on more open maps. The bridge-based layout meant that map control was won one bridge at a time, creating a strategic chess-like dynamic. The limited expansion options accessible without crossing bridges added economic tension. The map slightly favored Terran due to the siege tank's dominance on narrow bridges, while Zerg had to find creative ways to break through the confined corridors. Plasma is remembered as one of the most character-defining space platform maps, where the tileset's narrow bridges were used to maximum strategic effect.
