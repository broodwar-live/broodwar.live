A 4-player desert map introduced to the modern competitive rotation in 2024, featuring unique pathing and expansion layouts that distinguish it from other maps in the pool. The desert tileset provides bright, clear terrain with excellent visibility, while the four spawn positions are arranged with rotational symmetry across the map's quadrants.

Hitchhiker's defining feature is its unconventional expansion pathing — natural expansions are accessible but require navigating around terrain features, creating opportunities for harassment along the expansion routes. The center of the map is relatively open, but flanked by desert ridges that can be used for army positioning. The third base options require committing further from the main, making mid-game expansion decisions especially impactful.

The map encourages active, mobile play styles. Its wide corridors and open desert terrain favor armies that can reposition quickly, giving Zerg and bio-heavy Terran compositions an edge in maneuverability. Protoss players must be strategic about when and where to take engagements, as the open terrain can be unforgiving for slow-moving death balls. As a recent addition to the ASL map pool, Hitchhiker is still developing its competitive identity, with players adapting their builds to the map's distinctive layout.
