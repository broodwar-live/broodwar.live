One of the most played maps in Brood War history and arguably the defining competitive map of the modern era. Fighting Spirit is a 4-player jungle map with rotational symmetry across four clock positions. Each main base connects to a natural expansion via a narrow ramp, creating a defensible chokepoint that anchors early-game build orders. The center of the map is a wide open area where large armies collide, ringed by cliffs that enable siege tank positioning and high-ground vision control.

Fighting Spirit has appeared in virtually every ASL season since the league's inception, and was a regular fixture in the final years of OSL and MSL as well. Its longevity in competitive play is unmatched — it has hosted hundreds of televised professional matches across all eras of Korean Brood War. Notable series on this map include Flash vs Jaedong showmatches and countless ASL playoff games.

The map is widely considered balanced across all six matchups. No single race holds a decisive advantage, which is a key reason mapmakers and tournament organizers have kept it in rotation for so long. Protoss players appreciate the ramp-protected naturals for fast expansion builds, Terran can leverage the central cliffs for siege lines, and Zerg benefits from the multiple attack paths for flanking and runby plays. Third base options are accessible but exposed, creating tension around the mid-game transition.
