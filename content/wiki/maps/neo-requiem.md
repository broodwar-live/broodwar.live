A 4-player twilight map from the late golden era of Korean Brood War, used in professional tournaments from 2009 through 2011. Neo Requiem is a balance-revised version of the original Requiem, with the "Neo" prefix indicating terrain adjustments made to improve competitive fairness. The twilight tileset provides atmospheric purple-blue visuals, while the four spawn positions are arranged with rotational symmetry.

The map became known for its remarkably balanced matchup statistics across all three races — a rare achievement that made it a favorite among tournament organizers. Each main base connects to a natural expansion through a ramp with moderate chokepoint defense, and multiple paths lead from each base toward the center. The center features a mix of open terrain and elevated positions that create varied engagement scenarios.

Neo Requiem appeared in the final seasons of OSL and MSL, as well as early ASL competition. Its balanced statistics were particularly impressive during a period when many maps showed clear racial preferences. The twilight terrain features enabled interesting tactical play — raised platforms provided vision advantages, while lowered passages created alternate routes for flanking. The map rewarded adaptable players who could read their opponent's strategy and adjust accordingly, rather than favoring any single play style or racial composition.
