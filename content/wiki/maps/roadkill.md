A classic 4-player badlands map from the early golden era of Korean Brood War, used in professional tournaments from 2003 through 2006. Roadkill features rotational symmetry with four spawn positions connected by long ground paths. The map's defining characteristics are its tight choke points near each base and the considerable rush distances between spawns, which together strongly favored defensive, macro-oriented play styles.

The tight chokes near the natural expansions made early aggression difficult to execute, as defenders could hold with relatively small forces. This pushed the competitive meta on Roadkill toward patient, economic play — players would safely expand behind their chokes and build up large armies for decisive late-game engagements. The badlands terrain provided open ground between the choke-protected base areas, creating dramatic battles when armies finally met in the center.

Roadkill appeared in early OSL seasons and was a fixture of the mid-2000s competitive scene. The map's defensive nature meant that Protoss could safely reach their powerful late-game compositions, while Zerg had time to establish multiple hatcheries and tech to hive. Terran mech builds excelled, as the tight chokes were perfect for siege tank containment. The map is remembered as representative of its era's design philosophy, which favored longer, more strategic games over the aggressive styles that later maps would encourage.
