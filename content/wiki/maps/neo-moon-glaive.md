A reworked version of the original Moon Glaive with improved balance, serving as a staple of mid-2000s Korean professional play from 2005 through 2007. Neo Moon Glaive is a 4-player jungle map with rotational symmetry, where the "Neo" revision addressed matchup imbalances identified during the original map's competitive life. The jungle tileset provides varied terrain with cliffs, ramps, and open clearings.

The original Moon Glaive was a well-liked competitive map that suffered from specific positional or matchup issues. The Neo version refined the terrain around key areas — particularly expansion ramps, central passages, and cliff positions — to create a more even playing field. Each main base connects to a natural expansion through a ramp choke, while the center of the map features open terrain suitable for large army engagements.

Neo Moon Glaive appeared extensively in OSL and MSL seasons during the mid-2000s, hosting numerous important professional matches. The map produced well-rounded games that tested player fundamentals across all matchups. The jungle terrain provided opportunities for cliff-based harassment and drop play, while the balanced pathing meant no single strategy was dominant. The map is remembered as a successful example of iterative map design during the period when Korean mapmakers were perfecting the art of competitive map balance.
