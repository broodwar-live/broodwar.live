A 4-player space platform map that was one of the most important tournament maps during the golden age of Korean Brood War. Spawns sit at the 2, 4, 8, and 10 o'clock positions with rotational symmetry. Andromeda features spacious main bases that allow for complex tech builds, ramp-protected natural expansions with good chokepoint defense, and a relatively open central area connected by wide corridors along the space platform bridges.

The map was a major fixture in both the OnGameNet Starleague (OSL) and MBC StarCraft League (MSL) from 2008 through 2012, and has appeared periodically in ASL seasons as well. Some of the most legendary professional matches in BW history were played on Andromeda, making it one of the most storied maps in the game's competitive legacy. The space platform aesthetic with its dark bridges and void between platforms creates dramatic visual moments during gameplay.

Andromeda is widely regarded as one of the most balanced competitive maps ever created. All six matchups produce competitive win rates, with no race holding a dominant advantage. The spacious mains give Terran room for tech buildings and drop play, the ramp naturals support Protoss fast expansion builds, and the open center with multiple paths benefits Zerg's mobility and flanking potential. Third base positions are available but contested, ensuring that games progress naturally into exciting mid and late-game engagements.
