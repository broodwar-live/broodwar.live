A 3-player jungle map with a circular layout that has been a fixture in modern ASL seasons since 2020. The three spawn positions at 4, 8, and 12 o'clock are evenly spaced around the map's perimeter, each with a natural expansion protected by a tight choke point near the main ramp. The center of the map is a large open battleground devoid of resources, forcing players to expand outward along the edges.

Sylphid's tight natural chokes are its defining characteristic, making the map favorable for Terran mech play — siege tanks positioned behind the narrow ramp can hold against much larger forces. Protoss players benefit from the ability to create strong wall-offs, while Zerg often struggles with the constrained natural unless they secure a fast third base along the outer rim. The 3-player format introduces spawn uncertainty, as cross-spawns create longer rush distances than adjacent spawns.

The map has seen regular use in ASL group stages and playoffs, where it tends to produce methodical, positional games. The open center rewards players who control map vision and timing attacks, as armies caught in the middle of the map have nowhere to retreat. The outer expansion ring creates a circular macro game where securing territory along the rim becomes the key to late-game dominance.
