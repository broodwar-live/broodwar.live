A 4-player twilight map from the modern ASL era, used in competitive play from 2019 through 2021. Persona features the atmospheric purple-blue tones of the twilight tileset with rotational symmetry across four spawn positions. Each main base connects to a natural expansion through a ramp, while the map's open terrain and multiple attack paths encourage active, aggressive play styles.

The map's layout prioritizes mobility and map control over static defense. The open terrain between bases means that armies can move freely across the map, creating opportunities for multi-pronged attacks and flanking maneuvers. Multiple corridors connect each quadrant, preventing any player from relying on a single chokepoint to defend their territory. This design philosophy rewards players who maintain map awareness and react quickly to their opponent's movements.

During its time in the ASL rotation, Persona produced dynamic games across all matchups. The open terrain slightly favored mobile compositions — Zerg's speed advantage and Terran's bio mobility were well-suited to the map's wide spaces. Protoss players had to be strategic about engaging, using the twilight terrain's elevated features for army staging before committing to attacks. The map is remembered as a well-designed addition to the modern competitive pool that encouraged interactive, entertaining games.
