A reworked version of the classic Jade map that became one of the most popular competitive maps in the mid-2000s Korean pro scene. Neo Jade was a 4-player jungle map used extensively in OSL and MSL seasons from 2005 through 2007. The "Neo" prefix indicates it was a balance-revised version of the original, with terrain adjustments designed to address matchup imbalances that had been identified during the original Jade's competitive life.

The map features four spawn positions with rotational symmetry on a 128x128 jungle terrain. Each main base connects to a natural expansion with a ramp choke, while the center of the map offers a mix of open terrain and elevated jungle features. The rework focused on improving pathways and expansion accessibility, creating a more balanced competitive experience across all six matchups.

Neo Jade was notable for its role during a pivotal period in Korean Brood War, when mapmaking was becoming increasingly sophisticated and maps were regularly revised based on professional match data. The jungle terrain provided cliffs for high-ground harassment, trees for vision blocking, and open clearings for large army engagements. The map played well across all matchups and is remembered as a strong example of how iterative map design — taking a good foundation and refining it — could produce excellent competitive maps.
