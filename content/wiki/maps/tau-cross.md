A 3-player ice map shaped like the Greek letter tau (T), making it one of the most visually and structurally distinctive competitive maps in Brood War. Spawns are at the 1, 5, and 9 o'clock positions, with the T-shape creating inherently asymmetric distances between spawn pairs. The ice tileset bathes the entire map in cool blues and whites, providing a unique visual identity that stands out from the more common jungle and space platform maps.

The T-shaped layout creates fascinating strategic asymmetry — depending on which two positions are occupied in a 1v1, the rush distance and available attack paths differ significantly. One spawn pair may face each other across a short corridor while another pair must navigate around the T's crossbar. This asymmetry means players must adapt their builds based on scouting results, adding strategic depth beyond what symmetrical maps offer.

Tau Cross was used in professional tournaments from 2009 through 2014, primarily in late OSL/MSL seasons and early ASL competition. The map's asymmetric spawn dynamics created different games depending on positions, with some pairings favoring aggressive play and others encouraging macro development. The ice terrain features slippery-looking bridges and narrow passages that create natural chokepoints. The map is remembered for its bold design choices — the asymmetric T-shape was a departure from the rotational symmetry that dominated competitive map design.
