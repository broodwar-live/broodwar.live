A 4-player space platform map from the modern era, used in ASL competition from 2019 through 2021. Icarus features the dark metallic aesthetic of the space platform tileset with unique elevation changes and island expansions that set it apart from other maps in the competitive pool. The four spawn positions are arranged with rotational symmetry, each offering a main base connected to a natural expansion via platform bridges.

Named after the Greek mythological figure who flew too close to the sun, Icarus rewards bold but calculated play. The map's defining features are its elevation changes — platforms at different heights create dramatic high-ground advantages and vision asymmetries that profoundly affect army engagements. The island expansions, accessible only by air transport, add an economic dimension that becomes critical in the late game, as players who can control and harvest from these remote locations gain a significant resource advantage.

The map produced varied and exciting games during its ASL tenure. The elevation changes favored races with strong high-ground options — Terran siege tanks could dominate from elevated platforms, while Protoss reavers delivered devastating shots from above. Zerg had to be aggressive to prevent opponents from establishing unassailable positions on the high ground. The island expansions created interesting late-game dynamics where air control became essential, making units like carriers, battlecruisers, and guardians more relevant than on most other competitive maps.
