A twilight-themed 4-player map in the current ASL competitive pool, named after the Dutch Golden Age painter Johannes Vermeer. Introduced in 2024, the map features rotational symmetry with four spawn positions and the atmospheric purple-blue tones characteristic of the twilight tileset. Each main base connects to a natural expansion via a ramp with moderate choke protection.

Vermeer's layout balances open terrain with carefully placed terrain features that create meaningful strategic decisions. The center of the map includes contested areas that reward aggressive map control, while the outer edges provide safer expansion opportunities for players pursuing macro-oriented strategies. The twilight tileset's raised and lowered terrain creates interesting high-ground dynamics that affect army movement and vision.

As one of the newer maps in the competitive rotation, Vermeer is still building its competitive history. Early tournament results suggest the map plays well across all matchups, with no single race holding a dominant advantage. The aesthetic quality of the twilight tileset combined with the strategic depth of the layout has made it a popular pick among both players and viewers in ASL broadcasts.
