A 3-player jungle map with distinctive terrain features that has become a mainstay of modern ASL and BSL tournaments since 2019. The three spawn positions at 5, 8, and 12 o'clock are arranged in a triangular pattern, with each position offering a main base connected to a natural expansion through a ramp choke. The jungle tileset provides lush terrain with cliffs, trees, and elevated ridges that create natural defensive positions and vision blockers.

Aztec's layout creates asymmetric dynamics depending on spawn positions — the distances between each pair of spawns differ, meaning cross-map spawns face longer rush distances while adjacent spawns can be more aggressive early. This asymmetry adds a layer of strategic uncertainty that keeps games fresh. The center of the map is an open area where armies converge, with surrounding high ground that rewards players who control the terrain before engaging.

The map is known for producing exciting, action-packed games. The 3-player format introduces interesting mind games around scouting and spawn identification. In terms of matchup balance, the map plays relatively evenly, though the varied distances between spawns can affect specific matchups depending on positions. Protoss can leverage the ramp chokes for defensive openings, while Zerg benefits from the map's size when cross-spawned. Terran finds good siege positions on the elevated terrain near the center.
