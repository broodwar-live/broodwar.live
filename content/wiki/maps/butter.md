A 2-player desert map with a vertical layout that has become a staple decider map in modern ASL tournament sets. Spawns sit at the top and bottom of the map, connected by a central corridor flanked by open terrain on both sides. The natural expansions are positioned near the main ramps with moderate choke protection, while third base options extend laterally, creating wide flanking opportunities for aggressive players.

The open center of Butter encourages large-scale engagements and punishes overly passive play. The desert tileset provides clear visual terrain, and the relatively short rush distance means early aggression is always a threat. As a 2-player map, it eliminates the spawn randomness inherent in 4-player maps, making it a popular choice for deciding games in best-of series.

In terms of matchup balance, Butter plays fairly evenly across most matchups. The open terrain slightly favors mobile compositions — Zerg mutalisk harass can exploit the wide attack angles, while Terran bio armies benefit from the room to maneuver. Protoss players must be careful about committing to a single path, as the lateral expansions create vulnerability to multi-pronged attacks. The map has produced several exciting ASL finals games since its introduction to the competitive pool.
