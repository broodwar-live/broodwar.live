A legendary 4-player jungle map with rotational symmetry and an elevated central plateau that defined competitive Brood War during the golden age of Korean pro leagues. Spawns are uniquely positioned at 1, 2, 7, and 8 o'clock — paired closely at opposite corners rather than evenly distributed. Each main base features a large buildable area, and the ramp-protected natural expansions offer safe early game economic development. The central plateau serves as both a strategic control point and a high-ground staging area for armies.

Python was a cornerstone map of both the OnGameNet Starleague (OSL) and MBC StarCraft League (MSL) throughout the late 2000s. The 1.3 revision became the standard competitive version, with subtle terrain adjustments that improved matchup balance. Some of the most famous professional games in BW history were played on Python, including pivotal finals matches featuring players like Flash, Jaedong, and Bisu.

The map's design encourages diverse strategies across all matchups. The large mains give Terran room for tech-heavy builds, the ramp naturals let Protoss execute safe fast expansions, and the open center with multiple attack paths benefits Zerg's mobility. Third base locations are accessible but require committing army presence, creating natural tension in the mid-game. Python is widely considered one of the greatest competitive maps ever designed for Brood War.
