A 4-player badlands map in the current ASL competitive rotation that places heavy emphasis on positional play and terrain control. Introduced in 2024, Eddy features the rugged brown-red landscape of the badlands tileset, with rotational symmetry across four spawn positions. Each main base connects through a defined ramp to a natural expansion, with the badlands terrain creating natural ridges and plateaus throughout the map.

The map's name evokes circular movement, and its layout rewards players who understand how to rotate armies across the map's multiple paths. The center features a mix of open ground and elevated terrain that creates dynamic engagements where high-ground control matters significantly. Expansions beyond the natural require careful army positioning, as the map's terrain creates multiple angles of attack.

Eddy has quickly earned respect in the competitive community for its strategic depth. The positional emphasis means games tend to develop methodically, with players jockeying for terrain advantages before committing to major engagements. Terran mech builds find excellent siege positions on the ridgelines, Protoss can leverage the terrain for favorable army trades, and Zerg benefits from the map's size and multiple paths for late-game macro play. The map produces cerebral, strategically rich games that reward preparation and map knowledge.
