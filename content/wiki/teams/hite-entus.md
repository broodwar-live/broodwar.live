Hite Entus was a competitive Korean Proleague team that operated during the heart of the golden age of professional Brood War. Sponsored by Hite Brewery (later merged into the Hite-Jinro brand), the team was a regular Proleague participant known for developing strong players and competing fiercely despite lacking the star power of the top-tier organizations.

## Proleague Years

Hite Entus competed during the most competitive period in BW history, when the Proleague featured a dozen well-funded teams battling for supremacy. The team earned respect through preparation and teamwork rather than individual brilliance. Their matches were often close affairs, reflecting a roster that could compete with anyone but rarely dominated outright.

## Key Players

The team's roster featured solid competitors across all three races. JangBi (Han Sang Wook) spent time with the organization before his later career achievements at Samsung Khan. Tyson was a Terran player whose aggressive style could produce stunning upsets. Pusan contributed dependable results in the Protoss mirror and cross-race matchups. The coaching staff emphasized fundamentals and preparation, producing players who were tactically sound if not always mechanically dominant.

## Beer Sponsorship Era

Hite Entus was part of a unique wave of Korean BW teams sponsored by beverage and consumer goods companies. The Hite brand leveraged the team's visibility in televised Proleague broadcasts to reach the young male demographic that dominated BW viewership. This symbiotic relationship between consumer brands and esports teams was a hallmark of the Korean BW era.

## Disbandment

Hite Entus disbanded in 2012 as the professional BW landscape contracted. The team's steady, professional approach to competition exemplified the depth of talent and organization that made the Korean Proleague the most competitive esports league in the world during its peak years.
