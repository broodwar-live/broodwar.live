eSTRO was the fan-favorite team of Korean professional Brood War — a squad defined not by raw dominance but by creativity, personality, and some of the most entertaining players to ever compete. Founded in 2005, the team carved out a unique identity in an era dominated by methodical, optimization-driven play.

## Creative Genius

eSTRO's identity was built around players who treated StarCraft as an art form. Park Sang Hyun (Nal_rA), "The Genius Toss," was the team's spiritual leader. His unorthodox strategies, creative builds, and willingness to experiment made every game unpredictable. Nal_rA did not play to optimize — he played to innovate, and his games were appointment viewing for fans who valued style alongside substance.

## Shuttle's Reaver

Kim Hyun Deok (Shuttle) was famous for his exceptional reaver micro, a skill that became his trademark. The reaver — a notoriously difficult unit to control effectively — became an instrument of destruction in Shuttle's hands, producing highlight-reel moments that fans still discuss years later. Shuttle's mechanical precision with Protoss shuttle-reaver drops was so distinctive that it elevated the unit combination into an iconic part of BW culture.

## Much and Team Identity

Much (Song Hak Soo) provided consistent Zerg results and balanced the team's roster. eSTRO's coaching staff embraced the team's creative reputation rather than trying to force conventional play, creating an environment where unconventional strategies could flourish.

## Legacy

eSTRO disbanded in 2010 after a relatively short run. Their legacy is outsized relative to their championship count — eSTRO proved that competitive StarCraft could be thrilling, creative, and deeply entertaining, not just efficient.
